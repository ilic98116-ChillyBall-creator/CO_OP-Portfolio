Comic Project

This video game I collabed with Miley Hanhminhs - @hanhminhs to make a a similar replica of the famous arcade game PAC-MAN. The code was created in open processing and we started off with a rouph draft of a simple background grid and design of the little ghosts. In our good copy we decided to take a step forward and have pacman move with his mouth opening and closing in a continuous loop using PI. We had our PAC-MAN have the ability to eat white pellets with the conditions of: if he eats all the white pellets he wins, but if the ghosts get him first you lose. He does have one advantage that is if he uses the 4 red palletes in all corners of the grid to eat the ghosts and get through all the white dots either but be careful we applied randomness to the ghosts so you never know whether they'll leave you alone of come get you!

The Sketchbook

The silent film was my very first project working with Adobe Premier where videography and editing came together to show a rapid 1 minute speed-run of the amazing Society 145 building. Edits included changing floors through the elvator, high security key chips and immense space for gaming and leasiure time on the main floor. The story I wanted to imply in this building was a sense of looping as I start the day opening the curtains in the morning and finishing the clip by closeing the curtains once night time has arrived. This sense of closure is what I valued when making this film as simply just pasting events from place to place didn't feel as intrresting, it required a rythm of purpose and stage of leaving your room, to travelling floors, exploring activities and ending where you started.

The 6 Iterations

In this project with only audio took a great deal of interview recordings and re-recordings of pick-up sounds to produce this short interview of immersed experiance in a porshe. Matthew is a 2nd year student at the University of Waterloo and enjoys his passion for cars since the beginning of his childhood. He explains throughout his interview that he is priviledged to aquire such amenities for his joy of driving but what he values more is creating his own independence and strive on working hard to build his own pathway of success. In short, what his parents have brings motivation to him to work harder and become better than what he already has. The audio clips took immense work as Matthew and I did 3 rounds of interviews to really get comfortable and have him deeply think about why he craves his passion. Then, as we drove the sounds inside the car were too muffled for the audio recording, and for the car to accelerate loud we had to be in a safe enviornment for faster driving. So we decided to get the loud pick-up sounds at an empty parking garage at 1am when no-one was around and the fun began. Majourity of the time I was outside picking up sounds and revving the engine to really grasp the story of the car coming to life.
