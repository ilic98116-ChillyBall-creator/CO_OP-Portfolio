The North Star

A key challenge in this project was to create a fitting visual identity that would attract a target audience between ages of 19 - 24
while shifting the perception of the product. Instead of positioning the drink a good associated with partying and intoxication, I aimed to reframe the brand as a relaxing, enjoyable beverage suited for a lesiure, cottage setting. This required careful consideration of color, tone, and logo design to communicate a balance between playfulness and calmness.

Research & Critial Thinking

Using Adobe Illustrator, I explored geometric structure, form and balance to refine the logo. I also experimented with varied color palettes that reflect both playful and relaxful tones to the viewer. Intentionally, I maintained elements of symmetry from the original logo to create an emphasise on brand carrying calmness and stability; reinforcing the brand positioning as a refreshing, relaxing beverage. These iterations guided my final direction by ensuring consistency, clarity and alighnment with the target audiance.

Packaging

After analyzing existing packaging and shelf placement in convenience stores; I identified areas for improvement in the product’s visual identity, user experience, and audience appeal.
The original relied on a busy visual style that blended into similar products such as White Claw and Smirnoff. It lacked a strong connection to the product’s flavor and distinct mood of the target audiance.
My redesign shifts the focus toward a more relaxed, cottage-inspired identity by simplifying the layout and introducing playful, flavor-driven visuals. This included refining the color palette, typography, and visual hierarchy to stronger emotional association with leisure and enjoyment rather than nightlife. These changes improve how the product is perceived at first glance, making it easier for the target audience to recognize and connect with the brand.

Products & Improvements

These mockups created in Adobe Express explore how the brand translates across product packaging; focusing on consistency, flavor identity, and visual clarity. Each variation maintains a cohesive system while using color and imagery to differentiate flavors, reinforcing a recognizable and unified brand experience.
Moving forward, I would refine the logo by removing the black outline to reduce the “sticker-like” appearance and create a more integrated, professional look. I would also reconsider my choice of Aquavit typography for secondary information, such as “0g sugar” and “5% alcohol,” to improve readability and overall visual hierarchy.

Ai Brand Ad

This AI-generated concept using Adobe Firefly,
explores playful brand storytelling by visualizing the cans as animated objects interacting with their environment.
It was used as a quick experiment to test how motion and narrative could extend the brand beyond static packaging. Prompt: “Make the cans run away from their environment and swim away into the water and try to escape the lake and all cans squeeze into the packaging.”
