The Main Goal

Hamilton Rage Room is one of the only locations in Canada where visitors can release stress through physical activity while also exploring wellness experiences. However, its current website presents several issues in visual design, usability, and content clarity.
While three initial pain points were identified, a closer analysis reveals how these design choices negatively impact the user experience and the business itself.
The goal of this redesign is not only to improve the visual aesthetic, but also to strengthen customer trust, increase engagement, and support business growth.
One key issue is poor text visibility. For example, the phrase “deals for even more breakable fun” blends into the background due to low contrast between similar colors, making it difficult to read. Secondly, the typography lacks clear hierarchy; important headings are too small and can be mistaken for body text, reducing scannability. Third, color contrast such as white text on a bright yellow background does not meet readability expectations and strains the viewers eyes.
These issues matter because many users may not fully understand what a rage or reiki room experience involves. Without clear communication and visual structure, users may feel uncertain about what they are purchasing, which can reduce trust and impact conversion.

Safety First Not Seventeenth

Another critical factor is safety. After experiencing the service myself, it became clear that users need immediate access to information such as what to wear, what objects are restricted, and how to follow safety protocols.
Currently, the website presents safety in a way that can be overlooked. While a playful tone fits the brand, safety communication should maintain a level of formality, especially when users are required to sign waivers and confirm age restrictions. Content hierarchy also weakens the message.
Safety information is buried deep within the image sequence rather than being presented upfront. Instead of prioritizing excitement alone, the website should balance showcasing the experience with clearly communicating expectations and responsibilities. Additionally, small details such as visible file names on images reduce the perceived professionalism of the business. While minor, these inconsistencies affect trust and credibility. Overall, safety should be clearly visible, structured, and consistently reinforced throughout the site. In an environment where physical risk is involved, strong communication is essential to ensure users feel informed, prepared, and confident before participating.

Refund Policies

Transparency is essential in establishing trust with customers. Currently, critical policies such as rescheduling and late arrivals are placed at the bottom of the booking page where they are easily overlooked. This can lead to confusion and miscommunication. Presenting these policies earlier in the user journey before checkout ensures users are informed, reduces friction, and supports a more trustworthy experience.

Site Structure Analysis

Before implementing improvements, I analyzed the website’s structure by breaking it down into a sitemap to identify overlapping, redundant, and overused links.
The first major issue was how rage room packages were organized.
Each package based on group size from one to four people had its own separate page and navigation path. This created unnecessary repetition, making it harder for users to compare options and increasing complexity from both a user and development perspective. A more effective approach would be to group all packages within a single, organized flow.
Secondly, the cart experience introduced too many additional actions. Users were presented with multiple links, including adding notes, promo codes, browsing options, and additional booking steps. This level of choice can overwhelm users and disrupt the checkout process. Simplifying the interface and reducing unnecessary options would create a more focused and efficient experience.
Finally, the navigation system contained an excessive number of sub-links, making it difficult for users to orient themselves. The structure can be streamlined by consolidating content and reorganizing key information into the homepage such as the service overview, call to action, product offerings, and the business’s mission creating a clearer and more intuitive user journey.

Improved Site Structure

Based on the sitemap analysis, I restructured the website to create a more streamlined and user-friendly experience.
First, the booking process was simplified by consolidating key steps such as selecting the service, choosing a date and time, and booking into a single, organized page. Users can then review their cart, apply discounts if needed, and are clearly presented with the refund policy before completing any purchase. After checkout, users receive confirmation through both email and text message, reinforcing clarity and trust.
Second, I grouped all booking options based on group size (one to four people) into a single page. This allows users to easily compare packages and reduces unnecessary navigation. All options lead to a unified booking flow, minimizing confusion and simplifying decision making.
Third, the navigation bar was reduced to focus on a clear call to action. Additional information about the business, including its mission and
eco-conscious initiatives, was consolidated under a “Who Are We” section. This creates a more focused interface while still providing access to important background information.

Redesigned visual Layout

The redesigned visual layout improves both clarity and engagement through a more consistent color scheme and refined typography.
I introduced a short video to help users quickly understand how the rage room experience works, especially for those unfamiliar with the concept. I also updated the visuals using AI-assisted imagery through Firefly to create a more dynamic and cohesive presentation. Additional purchasing options were reorganized to be clearer and easier to navigate, reducing confusion during the booking process.
To maintain consistency, both the rage room and reiki sections follow the same visual system, creating a more unified and professional experience across the site.

Redesigned
Safety Rules

The safety rules page was redesigned to improve clarity, readability, and overall user awareness. I introduced a more structured visual hierarchy and a formal tone to better reflect the seriousness of the information being communicated.
Bullet-point formatting was used to make the rules concise and easy to scan, ensuring users can quickly understand important guidelines before participating. Additional sections, such as required gear and parent or guardian responsibilities, were also made more visible to reinforce safety expectations.
These changes help communicate critical information more effectively while creating a more professional and trustworthy user experience.

Refund Policy

To improve transparency and reduce potential confusion, I made refund policies visible earlier in the booking flow. Instead of placing this information at the end, the “no refunds” notice is clearly communicated during scheduling, allowing users to make informed decisions before proceeding.
This small change helps set clear expectations and builds trust between the user and the service.

Packaged Services

In the original design, each package was separated into its own page, creating unnecessary navigation and making comparison difficult. I redesigned this using a card-based layout that brings all options into a single view when clicked like an icon button.
This allows users to easily compare pricing and time variations at a glance, reducing cognitive load and simplifying the decision-making process. The result is a more efficient and user-friendly booking experience.

Service details Before Purchase

To improve clarity before checkout, I designed a structured booking flow that presents key service details, pricing, and policies in a single view. Users can review what they are paying for while also seeing important notices, such as the no-refund policy, before proceeding.
Clear actions such as browsing alternatives, confirming the booking, and checking out are kept within the same page to reduce unnecessary navigation. This creates a more transparent and streamlined decision-making process.

Future Booking Process

Given more time, I would refine the experience by introducing subtle animations to reduce the flatness of the interface and better reflect the quality and energy of the RageRoom service.
I would also expand the product offerings by highlighting additional purchasable items, such as upgraded tools and breakable add-ons, to enhance the overall customer experience.
Finally, I would place greater emphasis on the Reiki and tarot services. What makes Hamilton RageRoom unique is the combination of rage and relaxation, yet the current design prioritizes the rage experience more heavily. Rebalancing the visual hierarchy and layout between these two offerings would create a more cohesive and distinctive brand identity.
