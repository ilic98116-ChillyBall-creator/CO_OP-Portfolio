Insightful
Interviews

In Waterloo, nightlife is important in helping students balance academic and social life. To better understand this experience, I conducted user interviews focusing on both motivations and pain points. The study explored why students choose to attend
or avoid clubs and bars, who they go with, and how external factors such as relationships and social dynamics influence their decisions.
These insights revealed key gaps in the current nightlife experience and informed my initial design goal: to create an app that improves the efficiency of planning a night out while fostering a stronger sense of community. This was especially important for students who may feel disconnected, such as international students or those new to the region.
As the project developed and further insights were analyzed (shown in the following section), this goal evolved to better reflect user needs.

Iteration
Grouping

In the iteration and grouping phase, I synthesized interview data by clustering recurring complaints, preferences, and suggestions into key themes. The initial categories included planning and triggers, preferences, pain points, social needs, and cost considerations. This process revealed patterns in user behavior and helped prioritize areas of opportunity for design.
key insight: Students who participated less frequently in nightlife provided the most impactful feedback. While more experienced participants focused on niche preferences such as music, crowd dynamics, and venue atmosphere, less frequent attendees highlighted broader barriers such as planning complexity, social coordination, and comfort. For these users, the value of nightlife was less about the specific venue and more about spending meaningful time with friends. As participation decreased, the importance of organization and ease of planning became significantly more prominent.

Prioritizing APP Requirements

To define the final version of the app, we prioritized features based on both user value and feasibility within the project timeline. Using a prioritization matrix, we categorized ideas into must-have and could-have features, allowing us to focus on what would deliver the most impact to users. Original
Design Layout

The initial design layout was developed as a low-fidelity wireframe to explore how key information could be structured within the app on Figma. It focused on displaying venue details, tracking the location of friends within selected venues, and enabling users to request help or coordinate with their group. This concept aimed to simplify communication and enhance group planning during nightlife experiences.

Final Design Layout

The final design layout was developed to reflect an intuitive user journey from login to interaction. Upon entering the home screen, users are presented with two primary actions: discovering venues and accessing safety-related features.
The venue experience is supported by categorized listings, imagery, and detailed information to enhance trust and awareness of each location. To further streamline planning and decision-making, an AI chatbot named “Clubby” assists users by recommending venues based on individual and group preferences, helping accommodate diverse needs within a social setting.

User Experiance Feedback

After receiving additional user feedback through testing, several usability issues were identified that required refinement. Key improvements included restructuring the navigation bar for better clarity, simplifying the notification system, and revising the home screen layout to create a more intuitive entry point.
One important insight was the inconsistency between interaction patterns. The design initially mixed swipe gestures and tap-based navigation, which caused confusion. As a solution, the goal was to standardize interactions by committing to a single, consistent input method.
Further refinements focused on visual usability. For example, a fully dark interface reduced visibility in real-world nighttime settings, so adjustments were made to improve readability and contrast. Additional changes included refining icon sizing and layout to create a more balanced and accessible interface.
Key takeaway: Consistency and clarity in interaction design were essential to improving user confidence and overall experience.

Final Design System

The final design system establishes a cohesive visual language across the application, ensuring consistency in both aesthetics and usability. This system includes a defined color palette, icon set, and UI components that work together to create a recognizable and intuitive user experience.
The color palette was selected to balance vibrancy and clarity, helping differentiate features while maintaining visual harmony. Icons were designed to be simple and easily identifiable, supporting quick navigation and reducing cognitive load for users. Consistent use of typography, spacing, and component structure allows users to move through the app with ease and familiarity.
By developing this system, the design becomes scalable and adaptable, allowing future features to integrate seamlessly while maintaining a unified Nightlife brand identity.

Original App Layout

Once establishing a design direction through research, the project became a collaborative effort where we combined our ideas around storyboarding, safety features, and user experience to develop the final product. The original design explored key features such as finding venues, safety tools, and interacting with friends. However, through testing, we found that the layout created confusion, particularly with the use of swipe gestures and inconsistent interaction patterns. In the final design, we simplified the experience by replacing swipe interactions with clear button-based navigation and reorganizing features such as friend lists, proximity maps, and notifications. This made the app more intuitive and predictable for users. I refined smaller UI details, including button consistency, text clarity, and layout structure, to better highlight important safety information. Overall, these changes resulted in a more user-friendly and structured experience that supports both social planning and user safety.

Final App Layout

In future iterations, I would further incorporate user feedback by reinforcing user actions with clearer responses or rewards, helping users feel more confident in their decisions. While the current design includes a unique approach where the "Nightlife" text functions as a home button, this can create confusion for first-time users. To improve clarity and usability, I would prioritize implementing a more conventional navigation bar as a group decision. This would provide clearer orientation within the app while still allowing room to maintain unique design elements.
